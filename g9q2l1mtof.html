1. Electron Configuration is the distribution of electrons on the main energy level (shells) and sub-energy levels (sub-shells.)
2. Angular Quantum Number (l) is represented by an integer.
3. A p-subshell can hold a maximum of 8 electrons.
4. A f-subshell can hold a maximum of 14 electrons.
5. A s-subshell can hold a maximum of 6 electrons.
6. Quantum Numbers are sets of numbers used to locate and describe the location of the electron of an atom.
7. There are three quantum numbers.
8. Angular Momentum Quantum Number describes the energy of an electron in an atom.
9. In the Bohr model, the electron was assumed to move in circular orbits.
10. Aufbau Principle states that no two electrons can have the same set of the four quantum numbers.
11. Orbital box diagram are the pictorial representation of electron configuration.
12. The Pauli's Exclusion Principle, describes the order in which the electrons enter the different orbitals and sublevels.
13. The most stable arrangement of electrons have all the electrons with the lowest possible energy.
14. The s-orbital is represented as 1 box with a maximum of 2 electrons.
15. The f-orbital is having 7 boxes with a maximum of 16 electrons.
