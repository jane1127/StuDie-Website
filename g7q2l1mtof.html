1. Compound microscope is the most common type of microscope.
2. Galileo Galilei was credited with inventing one of the first compound microscopes.
3. A microscope primarily relies on mirrors, to magnify small objects or specimens.
4. Resolution describes how much larger an object appears when viewed.
5. Magnification is the capacity of a microscope to distinguish finer details of an image.
6. There are five major parts of the microscope.
7. Illuminating parts supply and regulate sound towards the specimen.
8. Magnifying parts make the specimen bigger or appear larger.
9. Tension Stress is the opposite of Shear Stress.
10. The science of investigating small objects and structures using such an instrument is called microscopy.
11. Objectives or objective lenses, this is the part used to look through the microscope.
12. Body tube or Lens tube is connected with the eyepiece and its main task is to hold it.
13. Base supports the microscope.
14. Diaphragm focuses images under the high- power and oil- immersion objectives.
15. Light source provides light for the specimen (could be a lamp or a mirror).
